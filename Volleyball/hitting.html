How-to Hit

A volleyball swing has many components. The wrist motions of the swing is arguably the most important part. The key to the motion is to snap the wrist around the ball. It sounds weird at first, but this snapping motion cause topspin which, when controled, is very powerful.

The core aspect of a swing is very important and many hitters overlook it. A tight core when shitting adds power with a natural snapping motion. This is power that also adds topspin. Any player can improve their hits just by remebering to tighten their core.

The shoulder in an arm swing controls the power of a shot. A faster should will hit the ball much harder. It is important to control this aspect of a swing. Too much power in the shoulder and a hitter will overshoot often.
